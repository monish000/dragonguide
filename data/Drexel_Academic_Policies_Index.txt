==========================================================================
REGISTRATION & ENROLLMENT POLICIES
==========================================================================

COURSE ADD/DROP POLICY
- Issued: September 2008 | Status: Issued
- Add/Drop period is the first week of each quarter.
- Last day to add/drop online via DrexelOne: Sunday of Week 1 by 11:59 p.m.
- Last day to add/drop with advisor assistance: Friday of Week 1.
- After the add/drop deadline, changes require advisor involvement.

COURSE AND CO-OP REGISTRATION POLICY
- Issued: September 2016 | Revised: March 2025 | Status: Issued
- Governs procedures for registering for courses and co-op placements.
- Students must agree to the Student Financial Obligation (SFO) before registering.

CLOSED SECTION OVERRIDE POLICY
- Issued: September 2005 | Status: Issued
- Students may request an override to register for a course that is at capacity.
- Override must be secured by Friday of Week 1 of each quarter.

ENROLLMENT AND ELIGIBILITY TO REGISTER POLICY
- Issued: September 2018 | Status: Issued
- Defines eligibility requirements for course registration.
- Students with holds may not be able to register until holds are resolved.

COURSE AND CO-OP WITHDRAWAL POLICY
- Issued: September 2008 | Revised: June 2023 | Status: Expedited Review
- Withdrawal window for quarter students: Week 2 to Week 7.
- A "W" appears on transcript but does NOT affect GPA.

LEAVE OF ABSENCE POLICY
- Issued: September 2018 | Status: Issued
- Students may take a leave for 1–4 quarters.
- After 4 quarters, readmission is required.
- Initiated by contacting academic advisor first.

READMISSION TO THE UNIVERSITY POLICY
- Issued: September 2018 | Status: Issued
- Students who left Drexel may apply for readmission through their college/school.

WITHDRAW FROM THE UNIVERSITY POLICY
- Issued: September 2008 | Revised: September 2018 | Status: Issued
- Must be initiated in person with academic advisor.
- Not possible to withdraw by telephone or verbal communication only.

REGISTERING FOR CLASSES DURING CO-OP POLICY
- Issued: January 2013 | Revised: May 2025 | Status: Expedited Review
- Governs whether/how students may take courses while on co-op placement.

GRADING POLICIES
==========================================================================

GRADES POLICY — UNDERGRADUATE STUDENTS
- Issued: September 2018 | Status: Issued
- Covers the grading scale (A+ through F), GPA calculation, and grade definitions.
- Minimum passing grade for undergraduate: D (1.0 grade points).

GRADES POLICY — GRADUATE STUDENTS
- Issued: September 2008 | Revised: September 2018 | Status: Issued
- Minimum passing grade for graduate courses: C (2.0 grade points).
- Grade of B- or below may be eligible for repeat/GPA adjustment petition.

INCOMPLETE GRADES — UNDERGRADUATE STUDENTS
- Issued: September 2020 | Status: Issued
- INC must be resolved per written Incomplete Contract with instructor.
- If not resolved by deadline, INC becomes I/F (permanent failing grade).

INCOMPLETE GRADES — GRADUATE STUDENTS
- Issued: September 2020 | Status: Issued
- INC may not exceed two subsequent terms without instructor/Graduate Studies approval.
- If unresolved, becomes I/F (permanent failing grade affecting GPA).

GRADE APPEALS POLICY
- Issued: September 2019 | Status: Under Review
- Students have ONE YEAR from grade assignment to contest a course grade.
- First step: Speak directly with the course instructor.

STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS FOR GRADES OR TRANSCRIPT CHANGES
- Issued: September 2008 | Revised: September 2018 | Status: Issued
- One year from separation (graduation, withdrawal, leave) to contest academic records.

REPEATED COURSES AND GPA ADJUSTMENTS — UNDERGRADUATE STUDENTS
- Issued: September 2008 | Revised: April 2024 | Status: Issued
- Only highest grade counts in GPA when a course is repeated.
- Students may repeat courses with a grade of C- or below.
- Since Fall 2024: No cap on number of courses eligible for GPA adjustment.

COURSE REPEAT POLICY — GRADUATE STUDENTS
- Issued: September 2008 | Revised: September 2018 | Status: Issued
- Graduate students may repeat up to 2 courses total.
- GPA adjustment available for courses with grade B- or below (once per course).

ACADEMIC STANDING POLICIES
==========================================================================

ACADEMIC STANDING — UNDERGRADUATE STUDENTS
- Issued: September 2008 | Revised: June 2024 | Status: Expedited Review
- Three standing levels: Good Standing, Academic Probation, Dismissed.
- Minimum GPA for good standing: 2.0.
- Academic standing reviewed at the end of each term.

ACADEMIC STANDING — GRADUATE STUDENTS
- Issued: September 2008 | Revised: September 2018 | Status: Issued
- Minimum GPA for graduate students: 3.0.
- Graduate students who fall below 3.0 may be placed on probation or dismissed.

DEAN'S LIST POLICY
- Issued: September 2008 | Revised: September 2018 | Status: Issued
- Full-time students with outstanding academic achievement recognized on Dean's List.

LATIN HONORS POLICY
- Issued: September 2008 | Revised: September 2018 | Status: Issued
- Cum Laude, Magna Cum Laude, Summa Cum Laude based on cumulative GPA at graduation.

GRADUATION POLICIES
==========================================================================

GRADUATION REQUIREMENTS — UNDERGRADUATE STUDENTS
- Issued: September 2008 | Status: Issued
- Requirements vary by program; consult your academic catalog year.
- Students must apply for their degree before the deadline each term.

GRANTING OF DEGREES POLICY
- Issued: September 1986 | Status: Issued
- Degrees are conferred on the conferral date listed on the diploma.
- Quarter conferral dates: Dec 13 (Fall), Mar 21 (Winter), Jun 13 (Spring), Sep 5 (Summer).

DUAL UNDERGRADUATE DEGREES POLICY
- Issued: September 2008 | Status: Issued
- Students may pursue two undergraduate degrees simultaneously.

DUAL MASTER'S DEGREE POLICY
- Issued: September 2018 | Status: Issued
- Graduate students may pursue two master's degrees under specified conditions.

UNIVERSITY CATALOG AND STUDENT DEGREE REQUIREMENTS POLICY
- Issued: September 2008 | Revised: September 2016 | Status: Issued
- Degree requirements are governed by the catalog year of the student's admission.

CREDIT AND ACADEMIC INTEGRITY POLICIES
==========================================================================

ACADEMIC INTEGRITY POLICY
- Issued: September 2008 | Revised: September 2016 | Status: Issued
- Prohibits cheating, plagiarism, fabrication, and unauthorized assistance.
- Violations can result in course failure, suspension, or dismissal.

ACADEMIC INTEGRITY PERTAINING TO ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE
- Issued: November 2023 | Status: Under Review
- Governs student use of AI tools for academic work.
- Students must disclose AI use per individual course policies.

CREDIT BY EXAMINATION POLICY
- Issued: March 2015 | Revised: September 2018 | Status: Issued
- Students may earn credit through approved examinations (AP, CLEP, etc.).

TRANSFER OF CREDIT POLICY
- Issued: September 2008 | Revised: April 2025 | Status: Expedited Review
- Transfer credits must be evaluated and approved by the student's college/school.

HIGH SCHOOL ADVANCED CREDIT POLICY
- Issued: September 2020 | Revised: May 2025 | Status: Expedited Review
- Advanced Placement (AP) and dual enrollment credits may be accepted.

STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS ON EARNED COURSE CREDIT
- Issued: September 2008 | Revised: November 2024 | Status: Expedited Review
- There may be time limits on how long earned credit remains valid.

CREDIT, NO CREDIT OPTION — UNDERGRADUATE STUDENTS
- Issued: September 2008 | Revised: September 2018 | Status: Issued
- Students may elect Credit/No Credit for certain elective courses.
- CR/NCR grades do NOT affect GPA but NCR counts against SAP completion rate.

UNDERGRADUATE STUDENTS TAKING GRADUATE COURSES
- Issued: April 2020 | Revised: May 2025 | Status: Expedited Review
- Senior undergraduates may be eligible to take graduate-level courses.

STUDENT RECORDS POLICIES
==========================================================================

FAMILY EDUCATIONAL RIGHTS AND PRIVACY ACT (FERPA) POLICY
- Issued: September 2008 | Revised: September 2009 | Status: Issued
- Governs the privacy of student education records.
- Students have the right to inspect and review their education records.
- Written consent required to disclose records to third parties.

STUDENT RECORD RESPONSIBILITY POLICY
- Issued: September 2008 | Status: Issued
- Students are responsible for the accuracy of their academic records.
- Must notify the appropriate office of any discrepancies promptly.

PREFERRED NAME POLICY
- Issued: September 2018 | Status: Issued
- Students may request a preferred name be used in university systems.

ABSENCE FROM CLASS POLICY
- Issued: December 2008 | Revised: May 2025 | Status: Expedited Review
- Governs student absences and instructor accommodation responsibilities.

RELIGIOUS OBSERVANCES POLICY
- Issued: September 2008 | Revised: September 2018 | Status: Issued
- Students observing religious holidays may request accommodations.
- Must notify instructor in advance.

FINAL EXAMINATIONS POLICY
- Issued: September 2018 | Status: Issued
- Final examinations are scheduled by the University Registrar.
- The exam schedule is posted each term.

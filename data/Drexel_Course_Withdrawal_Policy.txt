DREXEL UNIVERSITY — COURSE WITHDRAWAL POLICY
==========================================================================

OVERVIEW
Undergraduate students may withdraw from a course during the withdrawal period.
Withdrawn courses appear on the transcript with a "W" grade designation, indicating
the course was attempted but not completed. The "W" grade does NOT affect GPA.

==========================================================================
IMPLICATIONS OF WITHDRAWING
==========================================================================
Withdrawing from courses can have serious consequences:
- FINANCIAL: May affect billing and/or tuition refund eligibility
- FINANCIAL AID: May impact eligibility and Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) status
- VA BENEFITS: May affect veterans' benefits
- NCAA ATHLETICS: May affect eligibility to participate in NCAA athletic events
- INTERNATIONAL STUDENTS: May impact visa status (minimum credit load requirements)
- ACADEMIC PROGRESS: May impact ability to progress in major (especially prerequisites)
- TRANSCRIPT: "W" appears permanently on transcript

Students are STRONGLY ENCOURAGED to discuss their decision with:
1. Their course instructor
2. Their academic advisor
3. A Drexel Central representative
…before deciding to withdraw.

==========================================================================
HOW TO WITHDRAW ELECTRONICALLY (VIA DREXELONE)
==========================================================================
1. Log in to DrexelOne
2. Navigate to the Academics tab
3. Select "Register for Courses"
4. From new menu, select "Register for Courses"
5. Choose the current term from drop-down and select "Continue"
6. In the "Summary" window, find the course you wish to withdraw from
7. In the "Action" column, select "Web Withdrawal"
8. Select "Submit"

==========================================================================
RESTRICTIONS — WHEN YOU CANNOT WITHDRAW ELECTRONICALLY
==========================================================================
In these cases, use the Course Withdrawal Form (PDF) and collect signatures:

1. Graduate students in College of Nursing & Health Professions and Graduate
   School of Biomedical Engineering — must use paper form with instructor and
   advisor signatures.
2. Non-web-viewable courses (courses registered by academic department/college,
   e.g., COOP 101).
3. If withdrawal would violate visa requirements (international students).
4. If you have a hold that impacts registration (e.g., financial hold).
5. If withdrawing from ALL classes for the term — must speak with academic advisor.
6. ACADEMIC DISHONESTY cases: Withdrawal is NOT permitted. A withdrawal processed
   before a final academic dishonesty sanction is applied will REVERT to the
   instructor-assigned grade per the Academic Integrity Policy.

DREXEL UNIVERSITY — SATISFACTORY ACADEMIC PROGRESS (SAP) POLICY
==========================================================================

OVERVIEW
The Department of Education requires schools to monitor academic progress for
students to remain eligible for federal financial aid.

Academic progress is checked at the END OF EACH ACADEMIC YEAR:
- Quarter programs: Measured from September to September
- Semester programs: Measured from August to August

Failure to meet minimum completion percentage OR minimum GPA will result in
CANCELLATION of all federal financial aid for the upcoming year.

==========================================================================
FEDERAL AID AFFECTED BY FAILING SAP
==========================================================================
- Federal Pell Grant
- Federal Supplemental Educational Opportunity Grant (FSEOG)
- Federal Work-Study
- Federal Direct PLUS Loans (Parent and Graduate)
- Federal Direct Subsidized and Unsubsidized Loans
- PA State Grant (follows separate guidelines)

NOTE: SAP does NOT impact Drexel scholarships or grants (these have their own requirements).
Private loan holders should contact their lender about academic requirements.

==========================================================================
SAP REQUIREMENTS — GPA
==========================================================================
Student Level          | Minimum Cumulative GPA Required
-----------------------|----------------------------------
Undergraduate          | 2.0
Graduate               | 3.0
Law (JD) Students      | 2.2

Note: GPA requirements for merit scholarships may DIFFER from SAP GPA requirements.

==========================================================================
SAP REQUIREMENTS — COMPLETION PERCENTAGE
==========================================================================
Students must successfully complete a MINIMUM OF 67% of all courses attempted
since the start of enrollment at their current academic level.

Formula: Total Hours EARNED ÷ Total Hours ATTEMPTED

Grades that count as ATTEMPTED BUT NOT COMPLETED (hurt your completion rate):
- Incomplete (INC)
- No Grade Reported (NGR)
- Withdraw (W)
- F (or Fail in pass/fail course)
- No Credit (in credit/no credit course)

Grades that count as both ATTEMPTED AND COMPLETED:
- Transfer credits (if applied toward degree)
- Advanced Placement (AP) credits

NOT counted (neither attempted nor completed):
- Co-op placements

==========================================================================
SAP REQUIREMENTS — 150% TIME TO DEGREE
==========================================================================
You may NOT receive federal aid if you enroll beyond 150% of the published program length.

Example: 4-year undergraduate program → you have a maximum of 6 years to complete it.
After 6 years without a degree, you become ineligible for federal financial aid.

==========================================================================
HOW TO VIEW YOUR SAP STATUS
==========================================================================
1. Log in to DrexelOne
2. Select "Billing + Financial Aid" tab
3. In "Outstanding Financial Aid Requirements" channel, select
   "View Your Financial Aid Requirements History in BannerWeb"
4. Choose appropriate academic year and select "Submit"
5. Select the "Academic Progress" tab
6. SAP status appears in the "Satisfactory Academic Progress" section

Note: SAP information will NOT appear until all final grades for your first year
have been calculated.

==========================================================================
SAP APPEAL PROCESS
==========================================================================
If you are failing SAP due to extenuating circumstances (e.g., serious illness,
death of a parent), you may file an appeal.

APPEAL DEADLINE: Up to two weeks from the date you are notified of SAP failure.

TO FILE AN SAP APPEAL:
1. Complete the SAP Appeal Form (PDF) — available on DrexelOne/Drexel Central website
2. Attach supporting documentation for the circumstances described
3. Do NOT include medical documentation (diagnosis, test results) unless specifically requested
4. Submit to Drexel Central

IF APPROVED:
- You will be placed on financial aid PROBATION status
- You must complete an SAP Academic Plan (PDF) in coordination with your academic advisor
- The SAP Academic Plan must be returned to Drexel Central for financial aid to disburse

IF DENIED:
- You are ineligible for federal financial aid
- Consider applying for private loans or alternative funding

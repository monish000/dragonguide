HOW TO VIEW YOUR GRADES
==========================================================================
1. Log in to DrexelOne
2. Select the "Academics" tab
3. Click "Grades" in the "Grades, Transcripts and Records" channel
4. Select "Final Grades"
5. Select the term to view

NOTE: If there is a FINANCIAL HOLD on your account, you will NOT be able
to view your final grades until the hold is resolved.

==========================================================================
GRADE POINT AVERAGE (GPA) CALCULATION
==========================================================================
- Drexel computes a single, cumulative GPA for undergraduate and graduate work.
- GPA is calculated to two decimal points and is NOT rounded up.
- Only credits earned from the FIRST TIME a course is completed with a passing
  grade are included in earned/passed hours.
- GPA Formula: Sum of (Grade Points × Credits) ÷ Total Credits Attempted

==========================================================================
INCOMPLETE GRADE (INC) POLICY
==========================================================================
- An "INC" (Incomplete) may be assigned when work is not completed and the
  instructor deems the student CAN complete it within an agreed-upon time.
- A WRITTEN AGREEMENT (Incomplete Contract) must be created between student
  and instructor, specifying expectations and the completion deadline.
- The contract is processed electronically through DrexelOne.
- If the grade is NOT submitted by the agreed-upon completion date, the INC
  becomes an "I/F" on the record — this is treated as a FAILING GRADE and
  is calculated into the GPA as a permanent grade.

GRADUATE STUDENTS — INCOMPLETE GRADE SPECIFICS:
- Incomplete grades may NOT exceed two subsequent terms.
- Extensions require instructor approval AND Office of Graduate Studies consent.
- If a final grade is not submitted by the established deadline, the INC
  becomes an I/F (failing grade).

==========================================================================
GRADE NOT SUBMITTED (GNS) POLICY
==========================================================================
- GNS is recorded when an instructor does NOT submit a grade by the deadline.
- GNS is a temporary indicator, NOT a permanent grade.
- If unresolved by the last day of classes of the NEXT term, it becomes "N/F."
- N/F is calculated as a FAILURE in GPA and is a permanent grade.

==========================================================================
FINAL GRADE SUBMISSION DEADLINES (FACULTY)
==========================================================================
- Classes with fewer than 50 students: Grades due 48 hours after final exam
- Classes with 50 or more students: Grades due 72 hours after final exam
- All grades for a term are due by 12 p.m. on the Wednesday after the last
  day of Final Exam week.

Final grades are recorded in student academic history starting in Week 10 of
the academic term.

==========================================================================
GRADE CHANGE POLICY
==========================================================================
- Students have ONE YEAR from the time a final grade is assigned to contest it.
- To contest a grade: First speak directly with the course instructor.
- Students have ONE YEAR from the time of separation from a program to contest
  other actions or changes to their academic record/transcript.
  (Separation includes graduation, non-enrollment, withdrawal, or leave of absence.)
- Approved leaves of absence (medical, military) are handled on a case-by-case basis.

==========================================================================
ACADEMIC STANDING — THREE STATUS LEVELS
==========================================================================
Academic standing is determined at the CLOSE OF EACH TERM and takes effect
on the FIRST DAY of the subsequent term of study.

1. GOOD STANDING
   - Meeting all GPA and completion rate requirements.
   - Full enrollment rights and privileges.

2. ACADEMIC PROBATION
   - Placed on probation when not meeting minimum academic requirements.
   - Students must meet with their academic advisor to develop an improvement plan.
   - Continued non-performance may result in dismissal.

3. DISMISSED
   - Removal from the University for failure to meet academic requirements.

For detailed standards, see:
- Undergraduate Academic Standing and Probation Policy (Provost's website)
- Academic Standing — Graduate Students Policy (Provost's website)

==========================================================================
LATIN HONORS (UNDERGRADUATE GRADUATION)
==========================================================================
Latin Honors are awarded based on cumulative GPA at graduation.
- Summa Cum Laude:   Highest academic distinction
- Magna Cum Laude:   High academic distinction
- Cum Laude:         Academic distinction
(Specific GPA thresholds are defined in the Latin Honors Policy, Provost's office.)

==========================================================================
COURSE REPEAT POLICY — UNDERGRADUATE
==========================================================================
- Students may repeat courses in which they earned a grade of C- or lower.
- Only the HIGHEST GRADE is factored into GPA calculations.
- Students may enroll in a specific course up to THREE times (two lowest grades
  excluded from GPA).
- As of Fall 2024–2025: NO CAP on the number of courses that can be repeated
  using the GPA adjustment option (previously limited to 10 courses).

COURSE REPEAT POLICY — GRADUATE STUDENTS:
- Graduate students may repeat up to TWO courses total.
- Petition for GPA adjustment to count only the highest grade for courses
  in which they received a B- or lower.
- Limited to ONE petition per course.

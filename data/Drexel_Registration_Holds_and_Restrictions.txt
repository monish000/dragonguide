DREXEL UNIVERSITY — REGISTRATION HOLDS AND RESTRICTIONS
==========================================================================

OVERVIEW
If you are having trouble registering for classes, it may be because of a course
restriction or a hold on your account.

==========================================================================
REGISTRATION HOLDS
==========================================================================
Holds are placed on a student's account for different reasons and by different
University offices. Some holds will PREVENT future term registration until resolved.

HOW TO REVIEW HOLDS:
1. Log in to DrexelOne
2. Any hold on your account is listed in the "Academic Alerts" channel (top left)
3. The hold information will list a contact person to resolve the hold

TYPES OF HOLDS:
- Financial Hold: Must be resolved before the last day listed on the Academic Calendar
- Academic Hold: Contact your academic advisor
- Administrative Hold: Contact the specific office listed in the hold notification

==========================================================================
REGISTRATION RESTRICTIONS (ERROR MESSAGES)
==========================================================================
When you enter a CRN (Course Reference Number), an error message will appear
if there is a restriction preventing registration.

RESTRICTION TYPES:
- College Restriction: Only students from a specific college (e.g., College of Business only)
- Degree Restriction: Only students pursuing a specific degree (e.g., PhD only)
- Program Restriction: Only students in a specific program
- Program Level Restriction: Only undergraduate OR graduate students
- Major Restriction: Only students with a specific major
- Class Restriction: Only students of a certain class standing (e.g., Seniors only)
- Campus Restriction: Only students from a specific campus (e.g., Online students only)
- Student Attribute Restriction: Only specific students (e.g., Learning Community students only)

==========================================================================
COMMON REGISTRATION ERROR MESSAGES
==========================================================================

ERROR: CLOSED SECTION
- Section is full (maximum seating reached).
- Supersedes all other errors — other restrictions may be hidden behind this.
- Next steps: Add yourself to the waitlist, select another open section, or choose a
  different course. Check for other restrictions before joining waitlist.

ERROR: PERMISSION REQUIRED
- Instructor, department, dean, advisor, or program director permission needed.
- Statuses requiring permission: Department, Dean, Advisor, Honor's Program Director,
  Instructor, Instructor & College, Program Director.
- Next steps: Contact the relevant person/office to obtain permission.

ERROR: PREREQUISITE/TEST SCORE
- A specific course (or test score) must be completed before you can register.
- Next steps: Review the prerequisite list. Contact your academic advisor to discuss
  a prerequisite override if you believe you have equivalent preparation.

ERROR: TIME CONFLICT
- This course overlaps in time with a course already registered.
- Next steps: Select a different section that doesn't conflict.

ERROR: CO-REQUISITE
- Another course must be taken during the SAME term as this course.
- Next steps: Register for both courses simultaneously.

ERROR: LINK ERROR
- Course has multiple parts (lecture + lab and/or recitation) that must be taken together.
- Each part has a unique CRN but they form one complete course.
- Link identifiers: Lectures use L1/L2/L3, Labs use Z1/Z2/Z3, Recitations use R1/R2/R3.
- To fix: Register for parts with MATCHING link identifiers (e.g., L1 with Z1 and R1).

ERROR: MAX HOURS EXCEEDED
- Undergraduate students: Maximum 20 credits per term. Credits above 20 are billed per credit.
- Graduate students: Maximum 12 credit hours per term. Exceeding requires advisor and
  Graduate College approval.
- Next steps: Drop a course or consult your advisor to request an exception.

ERROR: REPEAT COURSES
- Non-repeatable courses require advisor assistance to register for again.
- See University Repeat Policy for more information.

==========================================================================
CLOSED SECTION OVERRIDES
==========================================================================
- A "Closed Section Override" allows you to register for a section that is officially full.
- You must secure the override by Friday of Week 1 of each quarter.
  - Fall 2025:   By Friday, September 26, 2025
  - Winter 2026: By Friday, January 9, 2026
  - Spring 2026: By Friday, April 3, 2026
  - Summer 2026: By Friday, June 26, 2026
- Contact the department or instructor to request the override.

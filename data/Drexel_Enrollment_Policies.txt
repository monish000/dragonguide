==========================================================================
DREXEL UNIVERSITY — ENROLLMENT AND REGISTRATION POLICIES
==========================================================================

OVERVIEW
Enrollment at Drexel University is determined by admission into a program and
attendance of courses or other official program activities (such as co-op).
Enrollment status is quantified by the number of credits registered for during a term.
Students on co-op are registered for co-op credits equivalent to full-time status.

==========================================================================
ENROLLMENT STATUS — UNDERGRADUATE STUDENTS
==========================================================================
Credit Load                  | Status
-----------------------------|-------------------------------------------
12 or more credits per term  | Full-time
9.0 – 11.99 credits per term | Three-Quarter (3/4)-Time
6.0 – 8.99 credits per term  | Half-time (Part-time)
Fewer than 6 credits         | Less than Half-time

==========================================================================
ENROLLMENT STATUS — GRADUATE STUDENTS
==========================================================================
Credit Load                  | Status
-----------------------------|-------------------------------------------
9 or more credits per term   | Full-time
7.0 – 8.99 credits per term  | Three-Quarter-Time
4.5 – 6.99 credits per term  | Half-time
Fewer than 4.49 credits      | Less than Half-time

==========================================================================
ACCELERATED COURSE STUDENTS
==========================================================================
For students with Part of Term (accelerated) courses, enrollment is determined by the
COMBINED number of credits from BOTH Part A and Part B of a term.

For financial aid eligibility:
- Students must be registered in BOTH Part A and Part B.
- Registration in only ONE part is insufficient for minimum part-time status,
  regardless of credits registered.

==========================================================================
MAXIMUM CREDIT HOURS PER TERM
==========================================================================
- Undergraduate: Maximum 20 credits per term.
  Credits above 20 are billed on a per-credit basis.
- Graduate: Maximum 12 credit hours per term.
  Exceeding 12 credits requires advisor approval AND Graduate College approval.

==========================================================================
FULL-TIME REQUIREMENTS
==========================================================================
- Full-time undergraduates: Must maintain at least 12 credits per quarter.
- Full-time graduates: Must maintain at least 9 credits per quarter.

If a full-time student drops below minimum credits during the adjustment period,
they are expected to add courses to maintain full-time status. If unable to meet
the minimum, charges will be automatically reassessed.

==========================================================================
FIRST-YEAR STUDENTS
==========================================================================
All new, incoming first-year students are provided with a class schedule for the
fall quarter. Course registration is NOT required — schedules are created by the
academic department.

Students are notified by their advising office when schedules are available on DrexelOne.
Questions or requests to update registration must be handled by the academic advisor.

==========================================================================
COURSE SCHEDULE AVAILABILITY
==========================================================================
Course schedules for the upcoming academic year are available starting in Week 3
of the prior summer term.
Available at: drexel.edu/webtms (Term Master Schedule)

==========================================================================
SENIOR YEAR EXCEPTION
==========================================================================
- Students entering senior year must apply for their degree.
- Full-time senior students in their FINAL TWO QUARTERS may register below
  the 12-credit minimum.
- These students are still classified as full-time for academic/institutional purposes.
- However, they will be BILLED ON A PER-CREDIT BASIS.
- They will be reported to the National Student Clearinghouse as less than full-time.
- Students must be aware of the Senior Exception for Financial Aid policy.

==========================================================================
LEAVE OF ABSENCE
==========================================================================
Students may interrupt their studies for academic, medical, financial, or personal reasons.

PROCESS:
1. Contact your academic advisor FIRST to initiate the leave of absence process.
2. Leave initiated DURING a quarter: Effective date determined by the Office of
   the University Registrar based on circumstances.
3. Leave initiated AFTER the last day of the term: Takes effect beginning next term.

DURATION:
- Minimum: 1 quarter
- Maximum: 4 quarters
- If maximum quarters reached, student must be officially READMITTED upon return.

==========================================================================
WITHDRAWING FROM DREXEL UNIVERSITY (PERMANENT)
==========================================================================
If transferring to another institution or leaving permanently:
- Must officially withdraw from the University.
- Process: Meet with your academic advisor to complete the University Withdrawal Form.
- During withdrawal, you will be counseled by multiple administrative offices.
- Drexel Central is the FINAL office in the process and will discuss financial implications.
- After Drexel Central consultation, withdrawal is processed and student record inactivated.

HOW TO INITIATE WITHDRAWAL:
- Must PERSONALLY report to your college or school to begin the process.
- If in-person is not feasible, write a request to your academic advisor using your
  official Drexel email account.
- Verbal notice or notice to anyone other than your designated college/school advisor
  is NOT sufficient.
- Withdrawal by phone is NOT possible.

ACCEPTED REASONS FOR WITHDRAWAL:
- Academic
- Career change
- Financial
- Medical
- Transfer
- Other/personal

EFFECTIVE DATE: Normally no earlier than the date of initial contact with the college/school.
The effective date determines the amount of refund and/or credit.

If you wish to return to Drexel after withdrawal, you must apply for readmission.

DREXEL UNIVERSITY — COURSE REGISTRATION GUIDE
==========================================================================

OVERVIEW
Course registration is an important student responsibility. Planning your schedule,
ensuring you remain on progress towards completing your degree, and selecting courses
that will aid in your success requires careful planning and attention to detail.

==========================================================================
STEP 1: EVALUATE YOUR CURRENT STATUS
==========================================================================
- DegreeWorks: Review your plan of study and progress to determine remaining requirements.
  Access via DrexelOne > Academics tab.
- Term Master Schedule (TMS): Determine what courses will be available.
  Available at: drexel.edu/webtms
- Academic Advisor: Meet with your advisor to discuss the upcoming term and progress.

==========================================================================
STEP 2: PREPARE TO REGISTER
==========================================================================
- Review Academic Policies affecting registration.
- Check Academic Calendars for important dates and deadlines.
- Review your Registration Time Ticket — the specific date/time you can register.
- Use Schedule Ahead (available from Week 4 through Week 1 of upcoming term) to:
  - Create up to 10 mock/sample schedules in advance
  - Note: Creating a sample schedule does NOT guarantee a seat in selected courses

==========================================================================
STEP 3: REGISTER FOR COURSES
==========================================================================
- Agree to Student Financial Obligation (SFO) Statement via DrexelOne before registering.
- Log in to DrexelOne > Academics tab > Register for Course
- Select "Check Course Availability," choose term, and search for courses.
- Review course restrictions before registering.

COURSE INSTRUCTIONAL METHODS:
- Face-to-Face (FTF): In-person at established times and campus space
- Remote Synchronous (SYN): Live remote sessions at established times
- Remote Asynchronous (ASY): Coursework completed remotely on own schedule
- Hybrid (HYB): Mix of in-person and remote sessions
- Online (ONL): Entirely online, typically for Drexel University Online programs

==========================================================================
STEP 4: CONFIRM YOUR ENROLLMENT
==========================================================================
- Confirm enrollment each term to verify your course registration is finalized.

==========================================================================
STEP 5: MID-TERM ADJUSTMENTS
==========================================================================
- Course Withdrawal: Available from Week 2 to Week 7 of a quarter.
- Leave of Absence: Available for students with extenuating circumstances.

==========================================================================
ADDING AND DROPPING COURSES
==========================================================================
SOURCE: https://drexel.edu/drexelcentral/registration/courses/adding-dropping

The add/drop period is the first week of the term.

Key Add/Drop Facts:
- Last day to Add/Drop with Academic Advisor assistance: Friday of Week 1
- Last day to Add/Drop via DrexelOne (online self-service): Sunday of Week 1 by 11:59 p.m.
- After the add/drop deadline, changes require academic advisor involvement.

SCHEDULE AHEAD FEATURE:
- Available from Week 4 of prior term through Week 1 of the upcoming term.
- Create up to 10 sample schedules before your time ticket opens.
- Having registration holds will NOT prevent use of Schedule Ahead, but WILL prevent
  actual registration until holds are resolved.
- Schedule Ahead does not check for course restrictions — verify separately on TMS.

AUDITING A COURSE:
- Provides opportunity to attend a course for no credit; grade shown as "AUD."
- AUD does NOT affect GPA.
- Audited credits DO count toward maximum term credit hours.
- Cannot fulfill major requirements using audit option.
- Must get approval from instructor AND academic advisor before add/drop period closes.
- Financial aid is NOT available for audited courses.

REGISTRATION TIME TICKETS — 2025–2026 ACADEMIC YEAR
==========================================================================
SOURCE: https://drexel.edu/drexelcentral/registration/courses/time-tickets

Time tickets indicate the specific day/time when you can register for classes via DrexelOne.
Your ticket is based on your student classification (credits earned).

HOW TO VIEW YOUR TIME TICKET:
1. Log in to DrexelOne
2. Select Academics tab
3. Select "Review Your Registration Information" under "Prepare to Register"
4. Select appropriate term and click Continue

SPECIAL TIME TICKETS: Athletes, ROTC, performing arts scholarship recipients,
veterans, veteran aid recipients, and Pennoni Honors College students receive priority.
